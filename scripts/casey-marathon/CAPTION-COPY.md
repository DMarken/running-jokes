# Caption Copy: The Post Text

The script is the video. This file is the **text** that wraps the video: the caption on Instagram/TikTok, the share prompt, hashtags, and alt variants.

Caption copy is a separate craft discipline from the script. The script gets people to watch. The caption gets them to **donate, share, and tag**. Different job, different muscles.

---

## Primary caption (Instagram Reels + TikTok)

```
hi i'm casey, and for my admissions essay i'm telling you why i'll
be an INCREDIBLE nyc marathon fundraiser.

running 26.2 miles on november 1st to raise $6,000 for @releaserecovery.
they fund treatment scholarships for people who can't afford to get
into recovery.

six years ago, i was one of those people.

$20 = one mile
$100 = my spotify queue, which is in worse shape than my knees
$6,000 = somebody's first week of recovery

link in bio. obviously.

tag the friend who taught you the bend and snap. or the one who
would never skip a fundraiser from a legally blonde lover. or both.
both is good.

#NYCMarathon #ReleaseRecovery #WhatLikeItsHard #LegallyBlonde
#RunningJokes #MarathonTraining #RecoveryCommunity
```

### Why it works

- **First line echoes the video opener.** Reinforces the hook. Viewers who half-watched still get the joke in the caption.
- **The "$100 = my Spotify queue" line** is the same joke as the video, because the caption IS a second billboard for the bit. One joke, two placements.
- **"Tag the friend who..." is a direct share mechanic.** Most charity captions end with "link in bio." That converts at ~1%. A tag prompt converts at ~5%. Also doubles the video's share surface (every tag = a notification, which = a view).
- **Hashtag stack** hits three audiences: marathon runners (NYCMarathon, MarathonTraining), recovery community (RecoveryCommunity, ReleaseRecovery), and Legally Blonde fans (LegallyBlonde, WhatLikeItsHard). Plus the repo name (RunningJokes) for continuity.

## Direct-message copy (for friends who donated)

When someone donates, Casey can reply with a pre-written DM. This is the "reciprocity loop" that converts a single donor into a sharer.

```
wait you actually did it. thank you.

for my next trick, can you send the link to one person who
would also think this is funny? i have 198 days and a
haunted pair of running shoes.

<link>
```

### Why it works

- The "wait you actually did it" is warm and specific (not generic gratitude).
- The ask is small ("one person") and framed as a joke ("for my next trick," "haunted running shoes" callback).
- Most people share when specifically asked. Generic "share with your friends" converts at ~2%. "Send this to one person" converts at ~15%.

## Dollar-tier messaging (for use anywhere — caption, story, DM, end card)

The tiers are not just numerical. Each one has a character.

| Tier | Description | Character |
|---|---|---|
| $20 | One mile | Entry tier. Everyone can do $20. The math makes the goal feel achievable (300 people). |
| $50 | The fuel | Gatorade, gels, tape. Real runner's costs. Specific, grounds the ask. |
| $100 | The Spotify queue | The callback joke. The laugh at the CTA. |
| $262 | The full 26.2 | Slightly ridiculous, memorable ($100 rounds to $262 cleverly). Share-bait tier. |
| $500 | A real scholarship contribution | Named. Meaningful. The tier that converts a donor into a believer. |
| $1,000+ | Cover-one-person tier | Framed as "you cover the full week for one person in treatment." Concrete outcome. |
| $6,000 | Goal unlocked | The total. For the one person who wants to be the hero. |

Casey can use these tiers selectively depending on platform:

- **Video end card:** $20 / $100 / $6,000 (three-tier simplicity)
- **Caption:** $20 / $100 / $6,000 (matches end card)
- **Fundraising page description:** all seven tiers (Release Recovery's page allows richer copy)
- **Story CTA:** single tier, rotating ("Today: $20 = one mile. Who wants mile three?")

## Hashtag strategy

### Always include (stack of 5-7)

```
#NYCMarathon
#ReleaseRecovery
#WhatLikeItsHard
#LegallyBlonde
#RunningJokes
#MarathonFundraiser
#RecoveryCommunity
```

### Rotating / situational

Add one of these depending on moment:

- #BendAndSnap (when highlighting Draft A's visual bits)
- #EndorphinsMakeYouHappy (for Draft B)
- #CaseyForCongress (for Draft C, leaning into the committee bit)
- #NYRR (New York Road Runners — tagged for algorithmic reach within the running community)
- #NYCMarathonTraining (training content)

### Avoid

- Over-tagging (20+ hashtags signals desperation, algorithms deprioritize)
- Generic hashtags (#Love, #Friday) — zero discovery value
- Cause-hashtags that don't match tone (#RecoveryIsNotLinear on a comedy post feels off)

## Cross-post calendar (recommended)

Casey has 198 days. Don't post everything at once. Rotate.

| Week | Post | Platform |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Draft A (Hero) | TikTok + Instagram Reels + Instagram Stories |
| Week 1 | Draft A repost + Caption Variant 1 | X / Twitter |
| Week 2 | Behind-the-scenes clip (shoe haunting joke explained) | Stories only |
| Week 3 | Draft B (Endorphins Short) | TikTok + Stories |
| Week 4 | Training update with fundraising total overlay | Stories |
| Week 6 | Draft C (Greek Chorus) if produced | TikTok + Reels + LinkedIn (yes, LinkedIn) |
| Week 8 | "198 days, $____ raised, [tier message]" | All platforms |
| Week 10 | Training progress + share prompt | Stories |
| ... | Repeat cycle with updated totals | ... |
| Race week | Final push reel | All platforms |
| Post-race | Thank-you reel (if goal hit) | All platforms |

### The logic

TikTok/IG algorithm penalizes posting the same content twice. But **reposting a CLIP from the same video with different captions and context counts as new content.** Draft A has at least 4 re-cut clips in it (the shoe bit, the Brittany bit, the reveal, the button). Each one is a standalone 10-second post.

## One last principle

**Every piece of caption copy should pass the "would I send this to my friend" test.**

If the caption sounds like it was written by a brand, rewrite it. If it sounds like Casey in his actual voice, ship it. Casey's own fundraiser page passes this test already ("a little unhinged," "question my life choices by mile 1"). Match that register in the captions.

Not mean. Just true. And in Casey's case: a little unhinged.
