# Draft A: The Hero — Admission Video to the Donor Committee

**Length:** 70 to 85 seconds
**Extras:** None (Casey solo)
**Best for:** Main reel / TikTok post
**Status:** Draft, awaiting Casey's review

## Format

Casey alone, talking to camera. Visual mimics Elle's Harvard video. Optional props: a pink binder or folder labeled "QUALIFICATIONS," sunglasses on top of the head, a clipboard. Shot outside or in a neutral setting. Dead calm delivery. No music under the voiceover until the button.

## The script

### Hook (0 to 3 seconds)

> This is my admission video to be your favorite New York City Marathon fundraiser. I'm Casey Shrader. Thank you for considering my application.

### Body

> My qualifications are as follows.
>
> I am running twenty-six point two miles on November first. Voluntarily.
>
> My anticipated finish time is four hours and twenty-two minutes. My body is not ready. My Spotify queue is less ready.
>
> I am raising six thousand dollars for Release Recovery. Release Recovery pays for treatment when a person cannot afford to get help.

### Subversion (around 62% mark)

> Six years ago I was a person who could not afford to get help. Somebody covered it for me. I am running a marathon to cover it for somebody else.

### Button (last 3 to 4 seconds)

> Twenty-six miles. What, like it's hard?

### End card / caption

Link in bio. Release Recovery. Every dollar equals scholarship access.

## Delivery notes

- **Calm, deadpan, slight amusement.** NOT pep-talk energy. NOT charity-ask energy. Read it like you're filing a form.
- **Pause after "Voluntarily."** One beat. Then keep moving.
- **The reveal line.** "Six years ago I was a person who could not afford to get help." Deliver this flatter than you want to. The delivery's flatness is what lets the content hit. If you emote, it tips to earnest and loses the Elle frame.
- **The button.** Straight Elle voice. Almost bored. Don't wink. The viewer winks for you.
- **Visual bit.** The binder/folder/clipboard is optional but recommended. Pink is a direct Elle signal without full cosplay.

## Why the inversion lands

The viewer hears "admission video" and expects a bit. They get a bit (specific self-deprecating running credentials) that builds sympathy. Then at the 60% mark, the frame flips: this wasn't a bit about YOU auditioning for attention. It was about you paying back a scholarship you once received.

The "What, like it's hard?" button then lands with opposite polarity from Elle's original. She meant Harvard Law was easy for her. You're saying the marathon is easy compared to what Release Recovery actually does for people.

Same line, reversed. That's what makes it shareable.

## What Casey might want to tweak

See [`CALIBRATION.md`](CALIBRATION.md) for the full list. Top three:

1. **Actual finish time.** "Four hours twenty-two minutes" is a placeholder. Use whatever your training pace suggests. Specificity is the joke. Accuracy is the credibility.
2. **"Six years ago" vs "Nearly six years ago."** Your fundraiser page uses "Nearly." You may prefer the softer version.
3. **Reveal line phrasing.** "I was a person who could not afford to get help" is direct. Softer: "Six years ago, I needed what they cover." Stronger: "Six years ago, I was the scholarship."
