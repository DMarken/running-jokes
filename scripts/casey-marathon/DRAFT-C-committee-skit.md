# Draft C: The Committee Skit

**Length:** 60 to 75 seconds
**Extras:** 2 to 3 friends as "admissions committee"
**Best for:** Highest ceiling if cast lands the deadpan. More production cost.
**Status:** Draft, awaiting Casey's review and casting

## Format

Casey is in running gear (shorts, bib, visible marathon aesthetic). Two or three friends sit at a table in business attire with folders, glasses, serious faces. Direct Elle "Harvard admissions committee" parallel. Comedic deadpan all around. Shot like a job interview or a Shark Tank scene.

## The script

### Title card (on screen 1 to 2 seconds — this is your searchable keyword delivery)

> ADMISSION VIDEO
> Casey Shrader
> NYC Marathon Fundraiser

### Opening

**CASEY** (standing, holding a clipboard): I am Casey Shrader. I am applying to be your favorite New York City Marathon fundraiser.

**FRIEND 1** (deadpan, not looking up from folder): Qualifications.

**CASEY:** I run a ten-minute mile. Sometimes. I have never run more than fifteen miles. I intend to run twenty-six point two on November first.

**FRIEND 2:** Training regimen.

**CASEY:** I tell people I am training.

*(Friends make a note. No expression.)*

**FRIEND 1:** Fundraising goal.

**CASEY:** Six thousand dollars. For Release Recovery. They pay for treatment when a person cannot afford to get help.

*(Small beat. Friends look up, not at Casey, at each other.)*

**FRIEND 2:** Personal connection.

### Subversion (around 65% mark)

**CASEY** (flat, holds eye contact with camera, not with friends): Six years ago I was the person who could not afford to get help.

*(Longer beat. Friend 1 closes the folder.)*

**FRIEND 1:** Application approved. Link in bio.

### Button

**CASEY** (to camera, same flat register): What, like it's hard?

### End card

> fundraisers.nyrr.org/casey-shrader

## Delivery notes

- **Committee friends: corporate-serious.** No smiles, no warmth. They break character ONLY on the reveal, and only subtly (closing the folder, looking up). No reaction shots of them laughing.
- **The tonal shift at the reveal is the whole thing.** Up to that moment, absurd interview. After that moment, real.
- **Casey's button to camera, NOT to the committee.** He's breaking the fourth wall on the viewer. Elle did this too. This is the detail that makes it land.
- **Wardrobe visual punchline:** committee in blazers, Casey in running shorts and a race bib. The contrast is part of the joke.

## Casting notes

You need friends who can hold deadpan. That's the whole bit. If someone smirks, it's dead.

Best casting: friends who can do dry/dead humor on command. Not your most animated friend.

Rehearse the reveal beat once. The committee's reaction (closing the folder, looking up for the first time) has to be choreographed or it won't hit.

## Why this version has the highest ceiling

Ensemble content is extremely shareable. The skit format is universally legible (Shark Tank, job interview, admissions committee all live in the same cultural pocket). Two to three extras make the content feel like a group bit, which multiplies the tagging/sharing instinct.

Risk: production cost. You need willing friends, coordinated wardrobe, and one rehearsal. If you can't get that, Draft A is the better move.
