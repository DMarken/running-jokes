# Draft A (v2): The Admissions Essay

**Length:** 60 to 75 seconds
**Format:** Solo-shot UGC mockumentary with jump cuts across 4 to 6 locations/outfits
**Production level:** Low (phone + a weekend + a pink headband)
**Best for:** Main reel / TikTok. The hero piece.
**Why v2 is different from v1:** v1 was Daniel-voice deadpan (observation mode). This is Elle-voice self-enhancing humor (affiliative mode). More jokes per minute, multiple LB anchors, hard CTA with dollar tiers. The jokes are what carry the ask.

---

## The opening principle

The first 3 seconds have one job: **stop the scroll using Elle Woods's actual admission video opener**, near-verbatim. People who know Legally Blonde recognize it in half a second. People who don't hear "admissions essay" and want to know what kind of admission is being essayed.

---

## The script

### Shot 1 — Home base (Casey on a couch or by a pool, in something pink, phone at arm's length)

**Casey:** "Hi. I'm Casey Shrader."

*(Jump cut, slight shift in position — classic Elle edit energy)*

**Casey:** "And for my admissions essay, I'm going to tell you why I'll be an incredible New York City Marathon fundraiser."

### Shot 2 — Jump cut, Casey on a running trail or track, mid-stretch, deadpan

**Casey:** "My qualifications are as follows."

### Shot 3 — Jump cut, close-up of Casey holding a well-worn running shoe

**Casey:** "I own six pairs of running shoes."

*(Beat. Slight flick of the shoe.)*

**Casey:** "They are all mildly haunted."

### Shot 4 — Jump cut, Casey on the track, hands on hips

**Casey:** "I run a ten-minute mile."

*(Half-beat.)*

**Casey:** "Sometimes."

### Shot 5 — Jump cut, Casey in the kitchen holding a protein bar like it's a legal document

**Casey:** "I know what electrolytes are."

*(Beat.)*

**Casey:** "Conceptually."

### Shot 6 — Jump cut, Casey pointing at a laptop or poster showing Brittany Runs a Marathon

**Casey:** "I have watched Brittany Runs a Marathon three times. I consider this cross-training."

### Shot 7 — Back to home base, slightly more composed

**Casey:** "I am running the New York City Marathon on November first. Voluntarily."

*(Beat. Small smirk.)*

**Casey:** "I need to raise six thousand dollars for Release Recovery. They fund treatment scholarships for people who cannot afford to get help."

### Shot 8 — Reveal (around 60% mark, delivery flattens, direct eye contact)

**Casey:** "Six years ago, I was one of those people."

*(One-beat pause. No music cue. Just the sentence.)*

**Casey:** "Somebody covered it for me. I am running this marathon to cover it for somebody else."

### Shot 9 — Mood lifts back to Elle-confidence (quick outfit change if possible, or just energy shift)

**Casey:** "Exercise gives you endorphins. Endorphins make you happy."

*(Jump cut, Casey holds up a pink placard that says "DONATE" in Sharpie)*

**Casey:** "Happy people donate to marathon fundraisers."

### Shot 10 — Final button, home base, flat confident Elle delivery

**Casey:** "Twenty-six miles."

*(Beat.)*

**Casey:** "What, like it's hard?"

### End card (on screen for 3 to 5 seconds with text overlay)

```
fundraisers.nyrr.org/casey-shrader
$20 = one mile
$100 = my Spotify queue
$6,000 = a person's first week of recovery
LINK IN BIO. OBVIOUSLY.
```

---

## Delivery notes for Casey

- **Home base aesthetic:** pick one anchor shot and return to it. Elle returned to her pool. You return to wherever feels most "you." Pink accent mandatory (headband, socks, shorts, placard). The pink is the visual hook.
- **Two registers to nail:**
  - **Elle-confident** (bright, performative, slight hair-flip energy) for the opener, the "qualifications," and the button.
  - **Flat and earned** for the reveal. "Six years ago I was one of those people." Do not emote. The line does the work.
- **Jump cuts are the comedy engine.** The gap between the sentence and the reality IS the joke. Deliver the line, edit immediately. No "bit" faces.
- **Protein bar as legal document bit:** hold it like you're Matlock. This reads instantly.
- **"Mildly haunted" shoes:** deadpan. Do not explain. Cut.
- **The endorphins line:** this is a direct Brooke Windham quote from Legally Blonde ("Exercise gives you endorphins. Endorphins make you happy. Happy people just don't shoot their husbands"). Remixing it to "Happy people donate to marathon fundraisers" is the second-biggest joke in the script. Nail it by delivering it as if it's the original, serious version — then the swap punches.
- **The button:** flat. Don't wink. The viewer winks for you.

## The LB anchor map (why this version earns the reference)

| Moment | LB Source | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| "Hi. I'm Casey Shrader. And for my admissions essay..." | Elle's opening line verbatim, barely remixed | Instant recognition hook for fans, curiosity gap for non-fans |
| "My qualifications are as follows" + jump-cut montage | Elle's admission video structure (pool → sorority → CPR → "I object") | Native cosplay of the format, not just the lines |
| "Six years ago I was one of those people" | Reveal that mirrors Elle's actual qualification (she WAS a sorority president, she COULD recite anything) | Same move: "the bit is also the truth" |
| "Exercise gives you endorphins..." | Brooke Windham, direct quote | Remix substitutes the punchline |
| "What, like it's hard?" | Elle to Warner, iconic | Button — reversed polarity (Elle meant it was easy for HER; Casey means the marathon is easy compared to the scholarship) |

Five anchors, not one. That's the craft difference between v1 and v2.

## Dollar-tier CTA rationale

Soft CTAs ("link in bio") underperform against specific-ask CTAs by 3-5x in fundraising content. Every tier does one job:

- **$20 = one mile** — entry tier. Low friction, symbolic, 300 of these hits the goal.
- **$100 = my Spotify queue** — callback joke that earns a laugh at the CTA moment (unusual, which helps conversion).
- **$6,000 = a person's first week of recovery** — anchors the goal number to the concrete outcome. Converts "abstract goal" into "person I am helping."

The joke in the middle tier is load-bearing. It's the only CTA Casey will post that ends on a laugh, which means more people read all three lines instead of bouncing on line one.

## Share prompt (for the caption)

"Tag the friend who would donate if you asked. Tag the one who wouldn't, too. They'll feel bad enough to click."

This is a "send-to" trigger. It gives the viewer a specific reason to tag someone, which is the mechanic that moves content from 10K views to 1M views on TikTok (share-to-view ratio).

## Humor classification (Martin)

- **Primary:** Self-enhancing humor ("unhinged marathon, finding the absurdity, inviting the viewer in")
- **Secondary:** Affiliative ("we're in this together — you know Legally Blonde, I know Legally Blonde, we're friends now")
- **Forbidden styles avoided:** No aggressive humor (no one is mocked). No self-defeating humor (Casey is the hero of his own story, not the butt of the joke).

## Benign violation check

- **Violation:** Casey's story (addiction, scholarship, dependency on a stranger's generosity) is real and heavy.
- **Benign frame:** The Elle Woods affiliative cosplay holds the weight. The jokes are specific. The reveal is flat and brief. The button is culturally pre-loaded.
- **Result:** The ask lands as a gift ("we're doing a bit together") instead of a guilt trip ("I need your money").

## Fallback shots (if time/energy is limited)

If Casey can only shoot ONE location, compress the jump cuts into costume changes at the home base. It works, just with slightly less visual dynamism.

Minimum viable shoot: Casey on a couch in pink, with four props stacked next to him (shoe, protein bar, laptop, placard). He picks them up one at a time and delivers the line. Total shoot time: 20 minutes.
