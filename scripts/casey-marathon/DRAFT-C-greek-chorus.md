# Draft C (v2): The Greek Chorus (Admissions Committee Remix)

**Length:** 60 to 80 seconds
**Format:** Skit with Casey + 2 to 3 friends playing the "committee"
**Production level:** Medium (casting, one rehearsal, coordinated wardrobe)
**Best for:** Highest viral ceiling IF cast can hold deadpan. Most labor-intensive.
**Why v2 is different from v1:** v1 had the committee as cold evaluators who approved Casey at the end. v2 upgrades them to Elle Woods's sorority chorus — they speak IN Legally Blonde quotes. The committee IS the Greek chorus of Legally Blonde itself. That's the layer v1 missed.

---

## Cast

- **Casey:** running gear, bib, pink headband
- **Committee Member 1:** blazer, glasses, folder, serious
- **Committee Member 2:** blazer, highlighter, clipboard, serious
- **Committee Member 3 (optional):** pink blazer, single perfect ponytail (she is the Elle of the committee)

All three committee members: deadpan, flat, business-formal delivery. They speak Legally Blonde quotes as if reading standard committee protocol. That is the joke.

---

## The script

### Title card (on screen 2 seconds, text overlay — delivers searchable keyword via text)

```
ADMISSIONS COMMITTEE
Casey Shrader — NYC Marathon Fundraiser
Case No. 26.2
```

### Scene opens — committee at a table, Casey standing in front

**Casey** (bright, Elle-nervous energy): "I'm Casey Shrader. I'm applying to be your favorite New York City Marathon fundraiser."

**Committee 1** (flat, reading from folder): "Qualifications."

**Casey:** "I run a ten-minute mile. Sometimes. I have watched Brittany Runs a Marathon three times. I consider this cross-training."

**Committee 2** (flat): "Training regimen."

**Casey:** "I tell people I am training."

*(Committee 1 makes a note. No expression.)*

**Committee 1** (still flat): "Fundraising goal."

**Casey:** "Six thousand dollars. For Release Recovery. They fund treatment when a person cannot afford to get help."

*(Small beat. Committee members glance at each other, then back to their folders. No smile. Tension holds.)*

**Committee 2:** "Personal connection."

### The reveal (around 65% mark)

**Casey** (Elle-energy drops, direct eye contact with camera): "Six years ago I was the person who couldn't afford to get help."

*(Long beat. Committee 1 closes the folder.)*

### Greek chorus activates

**Committee 1** (still flat, but looking up now): "Exercise gives you endorphins."

**Committee 2:** "Endorphins make you happy."

**Committee 3** (or Committee 1 if only two): "Happy people fund addiction treatment."

*(Beat. Chairs squeak. All three stand in unison.)*

**Committee 1:** "Application approved."

**Committee 2:** "Your cause is noted."

**Committee 3** (or 1): "We now ask the viewer to contribute."

### Final button — Casey to camera

**Casey** (flat Elle): "Twenty-six miles. What, like it's hard?"

### End card

```
fundraisers.nyrr.org/casey-shrader
$20 = one mile
$100 = keeps me off Casey's training-montage couch
$6,000 = the full goal
```

---

## Delivery notes

- **The committee is the joke.** They are not reacting to Casey. They are processing his application with bureaucratic flatness, using Legally Blonde quotes as official language. Zero smiles until the reveal. Even then, only the subtle click of a folder closing.
- **The chorus beat is the climax.** Committee members passing the endorphins line between them like they're reading from a policy manual — that's the set piece. Rehearse it. Get the rhythm right. It HAS to feel like "this is normal procedure," not "we are doing a bit."
- **Standing in unison.** Don't rush it. The chairs squeak, the committee rises. That's the visual punctuation. Film this in one shot if possible.
- **Casey's button is still to CAMERA, not to the committee.** He's breaking the fourth wall on the viewer. That's the seal.
- **Wardrobe contrast matters:** committee in blazers, Casey in running gear. Frame this in every shot.

---

## Why this version has the highest viral ceiling

Ensemble comedy is shareable at roughly 2x the rate of solo comedy (tag mechanic + "wait, that's me and my friends doing that" mechanic). The committee doubles as the "I need to tag my friends" trigger. If Casey has 2-3 friends willing to commit, this version can land.

Risk: if any committee member breaks character, smiles, or rushes the rhythm, the bit dies. Rehearse once. Shoot twice. Pick the best.

## Humor classification

- **Primary:** Affiliative (the committee and Casey are in on the joke together — the audience is invited in)
- **Secondary:** Self-enhancing (Casey's unhinged marathon, Elle's unhinged admissions video, the parallel is the premise)
- **Bonus:** The Greek chorus beat specifically is the highest-level affiliative humor — shared cultural text being spoken as if normal.

## Fallback: the chorus-lite version

If only ONE friend is available, compress to:

- Casey stands, delivers the opening.
- One committee friend reads all the questions.
- Casey delivers the reveal.
- The friend reads ALL THREE endorphins lines solo, flat, like reading from a script.
- Casey delivers the button.

Still works. Loses the chorus visual but keeps the joke.
